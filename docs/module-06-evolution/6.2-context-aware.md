In the last section, we saw the limitations of "Stateless" ([6.1 Simple Generative Chat](6.1-simple-chat.md)) AI. To solve this, developers created **Level 2: Context-Aware Models**.

## What is a Level 2 AI?

A Level 2 AI is a chatbot that has a "short-term memory." This is the version of AI you use most often today when you chat with ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

The key breakthrough was **Conversation History**.

Instead of only sending your current question to the AI, the application "pastes" all your previous messages together into one giant prompt before sending it to the model.

### The Lifecycle of a Level 2 Chat

1.  **Input 1:** You ask "Who was the first person to walk on the moon?"
2.  **Processing 1:** The model answers "Neil Armstrong."
3.  **The "Paste":** The application saves both your question and the answer in a list.
4.  **Input 2:** You ask "What year did that happen?"
5.  **Augmented Prompt:** The system sends a single, giant prompt to the model:
    ```text
    User: Who was the first person to walk on the moon?
    Assistant: Neil Armstrong.
    User: What year did that happen?
    ```
6.  **Processing 2:** Because of the [Attention Mechanism](../module-03-llm/3.2-attention-mechanism.md), the model can "look back" at the previous messages and realize that "that" refers to the moon landing.
7.  **Output 2:** It correctly answers "1969."

## Visualizing Level 2 in Python

```python
# A Level 2 AI has a "history" list to simulate memory
history = []

def level_2_chat(query):
    # 1. Add current query to history
    history.append(f"User: {query}")
    
    # 2. Build the giant context-aware prompt
    full_context = "\n".join(history)
    
    # 3. Simulate processing (The AI "reads" the whole context!)
    response = ""
    if "moon" in full_context.lower() and "year" in query.lower():
        response = "Assistant: That happened in 1969."
    elif "moon" in query.lower():
        response = "Assistant: Neil Armstrong was the first person on the moon."
    
    # 4. Add the AI's response to history too!
    history.append(response)
    return response

# First interaction
print(level_2_chat("Who walked on the moon?"))

# Second interaction (The AI "remembers" via context!)
print(level_2_chat("What year did that happen?"))
```

Running this prints:

```text
Assistant: Neil Armstrong was the first person on the moon.
Assistant: That happened in 1969.
```

## The Limitations of Level 2

While context-aware models are amazing, they are still "one-shot" thinkers. They receive a prompt, they generate an answer, and they stop. 

What if the model could stop, think about its own answer, and refine it *before* showing it to you? This leads us to Level 3.

**Exercise 2:** What is the main limitation of simply concatenating conversation history? What happens as the conversation grows?

**Answer:** As conversation history grows, the concatenated prompt becomes increasingly long. This consumes more of the model's [context window](../glossary.md#context-window) (Module 4), leaving less room for new information or complex instructions. Eventually, old messages may be "forgotten" if they exceed the window limit. This is why [RAG systems](../module-05-rag-tools/5.2-rag.md) (Module 5) are needed—to retrieve only the most relevant past messages rather than including everything.
