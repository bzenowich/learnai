# 6.1 Level 1: Simple Generative Chat

Now that we've covered the math (Module 1), the data (Module 2), the model (Module 3), and the context (Modules 4 & 5), let's look at how these pieces have evolved over time into the complex AI systems we use today.

We'll start at **Level 1: Simple Generative Chat**.

## What is a Level 1 AI?

A Level 1 AI is a **Stateless** chatbot. This was the earliest way people interacted with Large Language Models. 

"Stateless" means the model has no "short-term memory." It processes your prompt, gives an answer, and immediately forgets everything that happened.

### The Lifecycle of a Level 1 Chat

1.  **Input:** You ask "Who was the first person to walk on the moon?"
2.  **Processing:** The model uses its **Internal Weights** (learned during training) to predict the next tokens.
3.  **Output:** It answers "Neil Armstrong."
4.  **Amnesia:** If you follow up with "What year did that happen?", the model will have no idea who "that" refers to. It doesn't remember the previous message.

## Why Level 1 is Limited

While Level 1 models are impressive, they are extremely limited because they can't have a natural conversation. Every single question has to be self-contained and perfectly clear. 

They also lack access to any **External Context** (RAG or Tools). They can only answer questions based on the static information they learned during their training.

## Visualizing Level 1 in Python

```python
# A Level 1 AI has no history
def level_1_chat(query):
    # It just looks at the single query and responds
    if "moon" in query.lower():
        return "Neil Armstrong was the first person on the moon."
    else:
        return "I'm not sure what you're asking."

# First interaction
print(level_1_chat("Who walked on the moon?"))

# Second interaction (The AI has "forgotten" everything!)
print(level_1_chat("What year did that happen?")) 
# Result: I'm not sure what you're asking.
```

## Moving Forward

To make AI actually useful, we needed to find a way for it to "remember" what we said earlier in the conversation. This led us to Level 2.
